## Career

Xuzhou Wang is a Philadelphia-based AI product founder and systems engineer. He is pursuing a Master of Science in Systems Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania and previously earned a Bachelor of Science in Financial Mathematics from the University of Nottingham. Wang founded KitchenSurvivor, a multimodal generative AI platform addressing the "grocery-to-dining" crisis for overseas students. He architected a hybrid edge-cloud inference pipeline that decouples on-device optical character recognition from cloud-based language model reasoning, reducing cloud overhead by approximately 40 percent while improving time-to-value from seconds to milliseconds.

Prior to KitchenSurvivor, Wang co-founded an Amazon-affiliated e-commerce retailer startup where he led product strategy and growth initiatives. He designed a programmatic advertising optimization system that improved return on ad spend by 20 percent through semantic keyword clustering and dynamic budget reallocation. He also engineered a market intelligence pipeline using natural language processing to identify demand gaps and achieved rapid product-market fit. During a quantitative research internship at China Galaxy Securities, Wang productized a hybrid LSTM-XGBoost framework for automated trading, achieving approximately 33 percent annualized return through high-frequency signal generation.
